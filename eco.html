OUR SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT APPROACH

Eco Healing, an ambitious and voluntary approach

The Eco Spirit is first and foremost a way of life. The group champions a development model that respects people, the environment and local
communities. Under the ‘Eco Healing’ banner, a world of shared responsibility comes together to help shape the world of tomorrow.


Embodying the group’s approach to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR), 'Eco Healing' advocates a unifying vision. Its 360° ethics focus on all social,
economic and environmental aspects.

They are organised into six themes:

  1. Governance, sustainable innovation and dialogue
  2. A sustainable customer experience
  3. Responsible employer
  4. The environment
  5. Responsible catering
  6. Local development and solidarity

Because CSR is everyone’s responsibility and change cannot be implemented without the active involvement and commitment of our staff, Eco Healing
was co-created and incorporated into all the Group’s business lines thanks, notably, to the formation of dedicated Committees.

GREEN GLOBE
CERTIFICATION

.

In keeping with this approach, the Arcelia Hotel
have obtained Green Globe certification. Recognised by
the World Tourism Organization and the
World Travel and Tourism Council (WTTC), this
international label was specially created for travel and tourism
professionals almost 30 years ago to reward companies’ efforts
and unwavering commitment to sustainable development. The
selection criteria include management of sustainable development policy,
socio-economic commitments, respect for cultural heritage and
environmental preservation.

A SUSTAINABLE
CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE

.

In order to raise awareness about our
policy and the environmental impact of
the tourism sector among our guests, we are
introducing a protocol involving the non-systematic
replacement of towels and
bed linen in our hotel rooms.
When it comes to seminars and events, we have
also held ISO 20121 certification. With this in mind,
we implemented our Responsible MICE Charter
focused on the impacts of the events sector.
